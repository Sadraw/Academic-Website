## Introduction

This paper analyzes how uncertainty is constructed in discourse during internet shutdowns in Iran. It focuses on linguistic strategies such as hedging and speculation in order to show how meaning and authority are negotiated when reliable information is limited.

---

## Research Question

What differences can be identified in how state-controlled and independent media linguistically construct uncertainty during internet shutdowns in Iran?

## Data and Methodology

The data for this study consists of news articles from state-controlled Iranian media and independent Persian-language media operating outside of Iran but targeting Iranian or Persian-speaking audiences. The selected texts will focus on reports published during internet shutdowns in Iran.

All materials will be translated from Persian to English by the researcher for the purpose of analysis. This introduces an interpretive layer, which will be acknowledged in the analysis.

The study uses qualitative discourse analysis to examine how language constructs meaning in media. Discourse analysis focuses on how linguistic choices reflect underlying assumptions, power relations, and ideologies in communication.

---

## Analytical Focus

The analysis will focus on:

- Lexical choices (wording and framing)  
- Modality (certainty vs. uncertainty)  
- Agency (who is responsible or hidden)  
- Use of passive vs. active constructions
